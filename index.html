IV. Benchmark Curation & Procedural Methodology

4.1 Dataset Aggregation and Experimental Targets

To provide a robust evaluation of gaze-guided weakly supervised segmentation, we curated two comprehensive clinical benchmarks by integrating six distinct medical image repositories[cite: 267]. The Gastrointestinal Polyp Benchmark consolidates 3,784 endoscopic images from five widely recognized sources, including CVC-ClinicDB and PolypGen[cite: 270]. This task is characterized by high morphological variability and complex background interference[cite: 271]. The Neurological Neoplasm Benchmark focuses on T1-CE-MRI sequences from Nanfang Hospital and Tianjin Medical University, comprising 3,064 axial slices[cite: 272]. This testbed is designed to evaluate boundary-aware spatial reasoning in 3D medical contexts[cite: 273]. All images are standardized to a resolution of 384 × 384 for training and quantitative assessment[cite: 274].

4.2 Ocular Tracking and Quality Control Framework

We developed a non-intrusive acquisition framework to capture expert visual attention during routine image interpretation[cite: 278, 279]. This strategy replaces labor-intensive manual delineation with passive recording of diagnostic search patterns[cite: 279].

Clinical Protocol: Four board-certified practitioners participated in the study[cite: 280]. To ensure reliability, we employed a two-stage validation protocol involving independent reviews followed by a cross-expert audit to verify focus on lesion-relevant structures[cite: 301, 303, 304].

Technical Specifications: Gaze data was acquired using an HTC VIVE Pro Eye system integrated within a Unity VR environment[cite: 306, 307]. Calibration was performed before each session to ensure high-fidelity localization[cite: 308].

The sampling rate was maintained at 100 Hz[cite: 309]. Images were presented for a fixed duration of 2.0 seconds, resulting in approximately 100-200 raw gaze coordinates per sample[cite: 310].
